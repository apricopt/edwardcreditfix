How Billing Works

A one-time first work fee will be charged 5 to 15 days after signing up for our service for the preliminary work. We do not charge any fees prior to work being completed. The monthly fee begins 30 days after the customer pays for the first work fee. Our service is month-to-month and you may cancel anytime. You will be charged a final payment after cancellation since billing occurs after we already perform a month's service.

Creditfix Ultimate

Ultimate gives you access to all of the same resources and benefits found in Creditfix Basic and Creditfix Enhanced service levels. Customers will also be able to compare any of their pulled credit reports, as well as keep track of their monthly Transunion scores. Creditfix will also work on the customer's behalf to send cease and desist letters to overaggressive creditors, and increase the number of disputes sent out on your behalf if your case requires this.

Creditfix.com performs one or more of the following services before you make your first payment: Review and assess your current credit standings, and consult with you as to the information contained within your credit report; Enter all applicable data from your credit report into secure internal database; Your file is prepared for investigations and challenges to include the items that you indicated to be reporting erroneously per your instruction. Company then prepares and sends the individual investigations and/or challenges to each of the three major credit reporting agencies (Experian, Trans Union, and Equifax). First payment is charged for the initial work that is completed, which will be 5-10 days after you engage or services.

After, Creditfix.com in its sole judgment and discretion, typically performs one or more of the following ongoing and periodic services: Assist in the updating, correction, or removal of any item you have indicated has been reported erroneously or inaccurate; the conducting of periodic reviews and analysis of your credit status; continually receive and processing manual/automatic updates for you; responding to, receiving, and/or initiating correspondence via telephone, online, email, facsimile, or regular mail on behalf of you; creating a strategic plan to assist you in meeting your goals, upon your request; the creation of letters, emails, faxes, or electronic communications to the applicable creditors, collection companies, public record offices, and the three major reporting agencies in the attempt to correct or remove errors and other items appearing on your credit reports which you indicate to be inaccurate, incomplete, outdated, or unverified as per the Fair Credit Reporting Act on your behalf; the conducting of follow up via phone, email, or mail with you to discuss your status and strategy, providing assistance to you with credit/financial related questions to the best of our ability.
